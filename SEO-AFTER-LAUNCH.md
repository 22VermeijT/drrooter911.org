# SEO After Launch — Steps That Make a Huge Difference

This guide is for anyone managing Doctor Rooter 911’s online presence. You’re **replacing an outdated website with a new one at the same web address (URL)**. The business is **already listed** in places like Google, Yelp, and other directories. These steps are done **after** the new site goes live and will make sure those existing listings work for the new site and keep building your visibility. You don’t need to be technical.

---

## 1. Update Your Google Business Profile (Do This First)

**What it is:** The box that appears when people search “plumber near me” or “Doctor Rooter 911” — it shows your name, address, phone, hours, photos, and reviews. You’re already on there; the URL in that profile still points to your same website address, but the site behind it is now the new one.

**Why it matters:** Google uses this profile more than almost anything else for local businesses. Making sure it’s correct and up to date after you launch the new site helps people see the right info and land on the new site.

**What to do:**
- Go to [google.com/business](https://www.google.com/business) and sign in.
- Open your existing Doctor Rooter 911 profile.
- **Confirm the website URL** is correct (your same address, e.g. www.drrooter911.org). It doesn’t need to change — it’s the same URL, just a new site there.
- **Refresh the rest** so it matches the new site and is complete:
  - Business name, address, phone (must match the new website exactly).
  - **Category:** “Plumber” as primary; “Emergency plumbing service” if available.
  - **Hours:** 24/7 if that’s accurate.
  - **Services:** Update to match what’s on the new site (drain cleaning, sewer, emergency, etc.).
  - **Photos:** Add or refresh with a logo, truck, team, or job photos so the listing looks current.
- Whenever your phone, address, or hours change in the future, update this too.

**Result:** Your existing Google listing now sends people to the new site with accurate, up-to-date info. That’s one of the biggest wins after a relaunch.

---

## 2. Keep Getting (and Replying to) Google Reviews

**What it is:** Star ratings and written reviews on your Google Business Profile. You may already have some.

**Why it matters:** Good reviews make your listing stand out and help people choose you. Keeping the habit of asking for reviews (and replying) keeps your profile active and trusted.

**What to do:**
- After completed jobs, ask happy customers: “If you’re happy with the work, would you leave us a quick review on Google?” and send them your Google review link (from your Business Profile dashboard).
- Don’t offer money or free work for reviews — just ask.
- Reply to new reviews when you can (thank people for good ones; respond professionally to any negative ones).

**Result:** Your existing reviews stay, and new ones keep coming. That continues to help with visibility and calls.

---

## 3. Make Sure Your Name, Address, and Phone Match Everywhere

**What it is:** Your business **N**ame, **A**ddress, and **P**hone (often called “NAP”) should be **identical** on the new website and on every place you’re already listed (Google, Yelp, BBB, etc.).

**Why it matters:** Google uses this to recognize you as one real business. If the address or phone is different on one listing, it can hurt how you show up in local search. Now that the new site is live, it’s the right time to align everything.

**What to do:**
- Use the new website as the source of truth. Pick the exact format from there, for example:
  - **Name:** Doctor Rooter 911  
  - **Address:** 531 Lake Drive Rd, Edgerton, WI 53534  
  - **Phone:** (608) 244-9456 (and any other numbers you use)
- Go to each place you’re **already** listed (Google, Yelp, BBB, HomeAdvisor, chamber, etc.) and check that name, address, and phone match the new site. Fix any that don’t.
- The **website URL** stays the same — you’re not changing your address, just the site behind it.

**Result:** Google and other sites see one consistent business. That supports your local visibility.

---

## 4. Update Your Existing Directory Listings

**What it is:** You’re already on some directories (Yelp, BBB, Angi, chamber, etc.). Each one has your website link and maybe a short description.

**Why it matters:** Those listings already point to your URL. After launch, you want them to show the right info and send people to the new site (same URL — they’ll automatically get the new site). Refreshing the listing (description, services) can help too.

**What to do:**
- List every place you know you’re already listed (Google, Yelp, BBB, Angi/HomeAdvisor, chamber, etc.).
- Log in or claim each listing if you haven’t already.
- On each one:
  - **Confirm the website URL** is correct (same as always — e.g. www.drrooter911.org). No change needed unless it was wrong before; visitors will now see the new site.
  - **Update** business description or services if the form allows, so they match the new site.
  - **Check** name, address, and phone match the new site (see step 3).
- If you think you’re on a directory but don’t have access, try to claim it so you can keep it updated.

**Result:** Every existing listing now supports the new site. Same URL, new site — and consistent, accurate info everywhere.

---

## 5. Point People to the New Site (Same URL, New Look)

**What it is:** Your web address hasn’t changed — it’s still the same URL. What changed is the site itself (new design, new content). Letting people know “we’ve updated our website” can get them to look again.

**Why it matters:** More people visiting the new site (and calling from it) helps Google see that the site is relevant and useful. It also makes sure customers see your current services and contact info.

**What to do:**
- Add your website (and main phone) to email signatures, invoices, and estimates if it’s not there already.
- If you use Facebook or other social pages, make sure the website link is there (same URL).
- When it fits, tell customers or contacts: “We’ve updated our website — you can see our services and contact us at [your URL].”
- Optional: short voicemail line like “Visit our website at www.drrooter911.org for more info.”

**Result:** More traffic to the new site and clearer, up-to-date info for customers.

---

## 6. Keep Your Google Business Profile Active

**What it is:** The “Updates” or “Posts” feature in Google Business Profile — short updates, offers, or tips that show on your listing.

**Why it matters:** It signals that your business is active and gives you a bit more presence on the search page. Especially after a relaunch, a post like “New website — same great service” can help.

**What to do:**
- Log into your Google Business Profile every few weeks.
- Add a short post when it makes sense, for example:
  - “We’ve updated our website — check out our services and contact us 24/7.”
  - “24/7 emergency plumbing — call (608) 244-9456.”
  - “Serving Dane, Milwaukee, and Rock County. Drain cleaning, sewer service, and more.”
  - Seasonal tip or offer (e.g. “Get your drains checked before winter”).
- You don’t need to post every day; a few times a month is enough.

**Result:** Your listing looks current and supports the new site without any technical work.

## Quick Checklist (Copy and Use)

- [ ] Google Business Profile updated: website URL confirmed, name/address/phone/hours/services/photos refreshed to match the new site.
- [ ] Same name, address, and phone on the new website and on Google (and every other place you’re listed).
- [ ] Still asking happy customers for Google reviews; replying to reviews when possible.
- [ ] Every existing directory listing (Yelp, BBB, etc.) checked: correct URL, and description/NAP updated to match the new site.
- [ ] Website link in email signature, invoices, and anywhere else you communicate with customers (same URL as always).
- [ ] Plan to post on Google Business Profile a few times a month (updates, offers, or tips).

---

## What *Not* to Worry About (For Now)

- **Changing your web address** — You’re keeping the same URL. No need to change links anywhere; just update the site behind it.
- **Complex technical SEO** — The new website is already set up well. Focus on the steps above.
- **Buying “SEO packages” or backlinks** — Often low quality or risky. Updating your existing listings and keeping them accurate is safer and more effective.
- **Checking rankings every day** — Rankings move slowly. Check every few weeks or once a month.
- **Doing everything in one day** — Do step 1 first (update Google), then steps 2 and 3 (reviews and consistent NAP). The rest can follow.

---

**Bottom line:** You’re already listed in the right places at the same URL. After launch, the biggest wins are **updating Google Business Profile** so it reflects the new site, **keeping NAP consistent** everywhere, **refreshing existing directory listings**, and **keeping reviews and posts going**. Same URL, new site — these steps make sure the rest of the web works with it.
